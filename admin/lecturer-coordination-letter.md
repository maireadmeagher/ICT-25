# ICT for Scientists – Colleague Coordination Request

**To:** Lecturers teaching Year 1 Science modules (A, B, C, D, E)  
**From:** Ms. Mairead Meagher, ICT for Scientists  
**Date:** May 2026  
**Re:** Coordinating ICT skill delivery across first-year modules

---

Dear Colleague,

I hope this finds you well. I am writing to seek your help in improving the scheduling of the **ICT for Scientists** module, which all first-year science students take alongside your own module.

## The Issue

ICT for Scientists covers Microsoft Word, Excel, and PowerPoint — skills that students are also expected to use in their other modules throughout the semester. At present, there is a mismatch between *when* students encounter these tools in their other modules and *when* we teach the relevant skills in ICT. For example, students are sometimes asked to use Excel for data analysis in science practicals before we have covered it in class, which places an unfair burden on both students and their other lecturers.

## What I'm Asking

I would like to understand, for your module:

1. **Which Microsoft Office skills** do your students need? (e.g., Excel charts, Word report formatting, PowerPoint presentations)
2. **When** in the 10-week semester do they first need each skill?
3. **Are the skills needed throughout the semester**, or primarily by a specific deadline or assessment?
4. **Are there any minimum standards** you expect students to meet — e.g., a particular chart type, a referencing style, a report structure?

## How to Respond

I am sharing an Excel workbook with this letter. It has a tab for each module (A–E). Please find your module's tab and fill in:

- The ICT skills your students need
- The week they first need them (Weeks 1–10)
- Whether the need is ongoing or tied to a specific deadline
- Any notes or priorities

The sheet is designed to take no more than **10–15 minutes** to complete. Please return it to me by **[insert deadline]**.

## Why This Matters

Your input will directly shape the order in which I teach topics this year. The goal is that by the time your students need a skill in your module, they will already have covered it in ICT — so you are not left filling the gap, and students are not struggling unnecessarily.

I really appreciate your time and collaboration. If you would prefer to have a brief conversation rather than fill in the sheet, I am happy to arrange that too.
